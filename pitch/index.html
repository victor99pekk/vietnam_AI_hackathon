Why LLMs + Vietnamese

Powerful Models Need Native Vietnamese Understanding

  • The Power of LLMs: They serve as an adaptable operating system for human knowledge, scaling the ability to analyze, reason, and communicate instantly.
  • Cultural Precision: Native models naturally master Vietnam's complex tonal language and cultural nuances without losing meaning in translation.
  • Data Sovereignty: Keeping AI infrastructure local ensures sensitive citizen, corporate, and government data never leaves national borders.
  • Tech Autonomy: Owning the technology eliminates risky dependencies on foreign tech giants, pricing spikes, and geopolitical shifts.
  • Economic Boost: Tailored models can be directly optimized to accelerate local industries and power highly efficient public administration tools.

Conclusion: LLMs are powerful, but to make them truly useful for Vietnamese users, they must be trained on Vietnamese language and cultural data — that’s the motivation for our project.

Data Necessity

Vietnamese Data: An Urgent Need

  • Language fidelity: Only native Vietnamese corpora capture idioms, dialects, and cultural references accurately.
  • Cultural competence: Models trained on other languages miss local norms, values, and contextual meaning.
  • Equity & inclusion: People in smaller-language countries deserve models optimized for their language and experiences.
  • Practical impact: Healthcare, legal, education and public services perform better with localized knowledge.
  • Research sovereignty: Building local datasets reduces dependence on externally curated data and biased sources.

Why we built this: We thought this project was both fun and crucial — creating a high-quality Vietnamese knowledge base enables fairer, more accurate LLMs that truly serve Vietnamese speakers rather than forcing them to rely on systems optimized for other languages.

Training Stages

What data is needed in LLM training?

  • Pretraining: Massive, diverse, mostly unlabelled corpora — web text, books, articles, multilingual content to learn general language patterns.
  • Fine-tuning: Curated, high-quality Vietnamese datasets — instruction-following examples, aligned QA pairs, domain-specific documents, and safety-aligned samples.

Both stages require different mixes of scale and quality — pretraining needs breadth, fine-tuning needs depth and correctness. That means a training pipeline must supply large volumes of Vietnamese text plus smaller, verified, high-quality datasets with provenance.

Our Approach

Knowledge Graphs as a Foundation

  • Structured facts with provenance: Triples link entities to evidence sentences and source documents.
  • High-quality curated data: KG triples produce verified, de-duplicated records ideal for fine-tuning and evaluation.
  • Augmentation for pretraining: Serialized KG content and generated narratives increase high-quality Vietnamese coverage.
  • RAG-ready exports: KG-backed retrieval provides grounded context during inference, improving factuality and cultural sensitivity.
  • Interoperability: Exports to JSONL, GraphML, Neo4j CSV let teams plug KG data directly into training and evaluation pipelines.

In short: Knowledge graphs give us provenance-rich, high-precision Vietnamese data for fine-tuning and evaluation, while also enabling augmentation and retrieval strategies that improve pretraining coverage and runtime accuracy.

KG Characteristics

What We Focused On

  • Scalability: We store the graph in a graph database (Neo4j) to support growing nodes and relations.
  • High-quality data: We evaluate with metrics (completeness, consistency, duplication rate, factual grounding) and compare fine-tuning LLMs with KG data vs without KG vs no fine-tuning.
  • Data preservation: We keep historical versions of source documents while evolving the KG by iteratively adding nodes and evidence.
  • Continuous expansion: We built a legal domain scraper to feed more domain-specific text — LLMs are data-hungry and benefit from continuous ingestion.

These characteristics ensure the KG is robust, auditable, and ready to drive both model training and runtime retrieval strategies.

Pipeline

Overall Pipeline Architecture

  • Ingest: Collect TXT/JSON/JSONL/HTML, normalize encoding and whitespace.
  • Chunk & Clean: Sentence or semantic chunking, noise removal, language detection.
  • Deduplication & QA: Exact, MinHash/SimHash, semantic layers; quality scoring per chunk.
  • Extraction: NER + relation extraction (spaCy rules or LLM prompts) to produce triples.
  • Entity Resolution: Embedding similarity and canonicalization across documents.
  • Graph Build & Validation: Construct triples, validate against ontology, attach provenance.
  • Evaluate & Export: Compute quality metrics, export JSONL/GraphML/Neo4j CSV, and prepare RAG indexes.
  • Continuous Loop: Scrapers and manual curation feed new data; versioning preserves history while KG grows.

This modular pipeline supports both large-scale pretraining augmentation and fine-grained curated datasets for fine-tuning and evaluation.
