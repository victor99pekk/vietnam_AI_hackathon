# VAIC 2026: Official Scoring Guide & Evaluation Framework
## Vietnam AI Innovation Challenge 2026

This document compiles all official scoring criteria, submission requirements, evaluation workflows, and judging standards for the **Vietnam AI Innovation Challenge 2026 (VAIC 2026)**. Teams should use this guide to ensure their solutions align with the hackathon's multi-dimensional evaluation framework.

---

## 1. Core Submission Requirements

To be evaluated by the judging panel, all competing teams must submit exactly **five (5) required deliverables** at the end of the 48-hour hackathon window. The top 10 teams with the highest preliminary scores will advance to the live presentation phase.

### Required Deliverables:
1. **Presentation Slides:** Documenting the problem statement, technical architecture, business model, and product vision.
2. **Demo Video (≤ 5 minutes):** A recorded demonstration showcasing the main workflow and core value proposition of the functional prototype.
3. **GitHub Repository:** A public repository containing the source code developed during the 48-hour period.
4. **Live Administrator URL:** A working deployment or accessible interface of the product/prototype for live validation.
5. **AI Collaboration Log:** Detailed documentation of the team's interaction and collaboration workflow with AI tools, as mandated by the *100% AI-Native Oath*.

## 3. Detailed Breakdown of Scoring Pillars

### 3.1. Technical Implementation & Engineering Depth (20 Points)
Assesses the quality, soundness, and stability of the product's underlying engineering infrastructure.
* **Prototype Functionality:** The prototype must be fully operational and clearly demonstrate the main target workflows.
* **Architecture Logic:** The technical architecture must be logical, coherent, and appropriately scaled to the problem complexity.
* **Component Integration:** Effective and appropriate integration of frontend, backend, database systems, APIs, and data workflows.
* **Time-constrained Execution:** Demonstrable ability of the team to resolve complex technical hurdles within the strict 48-hour framework.
* **Scalability & Maintenance:** The codebase and infrastructure should exhibit clear foundations for expansion, continuous maintenance, or post-competition refinement.

### 3.2. AI-Native Architecture & Innovation (20 Points)
Evaluates the degree to which artificial intelligence serves as the structural core of the product, rather than an auxiliary feature.
* **Purposeful AI Integration:** AI capabilities must be woven into the very fabric of the solution, serving a distinct, critical purpose.
* **Advanced AI Capabilities:** Effective leveraging of core AI capabilities such as advanced reasoning, automated workflows, deep personalization, agentic workflows, retrieval-augmented systems, generation, or intelligent decision support.
* **AI-Native vs. Traditional Design:** System designs must reflect an AI-native structural mindset, moving beyond simple, traditional application layouts or baseline chatbot overlays.
* **Creativity & Uniqueness:** The solution introduces novel, unique, or highly creative paradigms to address the given track problem.
* **Efficiency Optimization:** Clear potential to dramatically improve efficiency, processing speed, output quality, or experiential depth compared to legacy methodologies.

### 3.3. Business Viability & Pilot Pathway (20 Points)
Measures the real-world commercial potential and logical progression from a hackathon prototype to a sustainable enterprise or startup.
* **Problem-Solution Fit:** Addresses a clear, verified, and practical market or societal problem with a demonstrable need.
* **Target Audience Identification:** Precision in mapping out the exact target users, customer personas, or key beneficiaries.
* **Value Proposition:** The solution's value must be presented clearly, tangibly, and convincingly.
* **Real-World Testability (Pilot Readiness):** High potential for immediate, controlled deployment and testing in a real-world operating environment.
* **Strategic Roadmap:** A well-considered future roadmap including a pilot phase plan, performance measurement metrics, and scaling strategies.
* **Model Soundness:** A logical, viable operational, collaborative, or business model formulated for the initial phase.

### 3.4. AI-Native UX & Design Thinking (15 Points)
Focuses on user experience optimized for human-AI interaction, ensuring systems build intuitive trust and fluid utility.
* **User Context Comprehension:** Demonstrates deep empathy and understanding regarding the target users' environment, explicit needs, behavioral contexts, and operational friction points.
* **Logical UX Flow:** Interaction pathways must be exceptionally clear, frictionless, and simple to follow.
* **Intuitive AI Interfaces:** Interface design or interactive modalities must be user-friendly, clean, and custom-tailored for managing AI outputs.
* **Explainability & Clarity:** Users must easily comprehend how the AI assists them and clearly interpret the contextual meaning of AI-generated results.
* **Decision Support Utility:** The product actively and effectively empowers users to make higher-quality decisions or solve domain-specific problems.
* **User-Centric Mindset:** Layouts and workflows stem from deep user-centric thinking rather than merely displaying technical features.

### 3.5. AI Safety, Grounding & Trust (15 Points)
Assesses the systemic compliance, structural safety mechanisms, and reliability built into the AI engine.
* **Hallucination Mitigation:** Robust structural mechanisms to minimize system errors, factual hallucinations, or ungrounded outputs.
* **Verifiability & Interpretability:** AI-generated outputs must be auditable, interpretable, or accompanied by verifiable source citations where applicable.
* **Data Security & Privacy:** Data extraction, processing, and handling protocols must strictly comply with legal privacy standards and data ethics.
* **Ethical & Bias Management:** Comprehensive mitigation strategies for handling ethical hazards, security vulnerabilities, systemic algorithmic bias, or extreme negative downstream impacts.
* **Transparency of Limitations:** The product explicitly provides sufficient visibility and information so users understand the clear performance boundaries and structural limitations of the underlying AI model.
* **Accountability Framework:** Architectural elements designed specifically to uphold high reliability and professional accountability in practical field operations.

### 3.6. Presentation, Demo & Defensibility (10 Points)
Measures communication performance, presentation structure, and the team's capability to defend their engineering and product decisions.
* **Structure & Focus:** Presentations must be exceptionally well-structured, focused, impactful, and easy to follow.
* **Core Value Demonstration:** The live or recorded demo must cleanly illustrate the absolute core value proposition and primary workflows without crashing.
* **Methodological Rationale:** Clear, data-driven technical and product justifications explaining *why* the team chose their specific approach.
* **Cross-Examination Defensibility:** High proficiency in addressing critical questions from the judges covering technical stacks, product design, target user logic, and deployment feasibility.
* **Competitive Advantage:** Clear articulation of distinct differentiating features and unfair competitive advantages over existing market solutions.
* **Persuasiveness & Mastery:** Full ownership and deep technical command displayed by the team throughout the presentation cycle.

## 4. The Evaluating Body: The Judging Panel

VAIC 2026 deploys a multi-faceted, diverse judging panel to ensure scores are balanced from technical, operational, and commercial perspectives. Teams will be graded by four distinct archetypes of judges:

1. **Domain Experts:** In-depth specialists who evaluate the contextual fit, market accuracy, and operational alignment of the solution with real-world industry pain points.
2. **Technical Judges:** AI/ML practitioners who analyze system architectures, code quality, integration models, technological appropriateness, and raw prototype stability.
3. **Non-Tech Industry Judges:** Representatives of end-users and corporate business units (e.g., HR, Finance, Operations) who evaluate ease of use, practicality, and direct user acceptance.
4. **Senior Judges:** Founders, venture capitalists, investment managers, and AI lab directors who look at strategic scalability, market valuation, and long-term startup potential ("Ends in a Startup" philosophy).

*Note: In all scenarios, the official collective decision of the Judging Panel regarding scoring tallies and placement ranking is final.*

## 5. Critical Rules Directly Affecting Scores

Failure to adhere to the following operational parameters can heavily penalize or entirely invalidate a team's score:
* **The 100% AI-Native Oath:** All core code and logic must be written entirely within the 48-hour event window. Submitting pre-fabricated products, plagiarism, or falsifying development timelines will trigger **immediate disqualification**.
* **AI Collaboration Transparency:** Teams must maintain an explicit *AI Collaboration Log*. Lack of transparency or failure to provide this log directly jeopardizes award eligibility.
* **Strict Submission Deadlines:** Deliverables must be committed and uploaded precisely in accordance with the technical deadlines on the official hackathon platform (`https://hub.aiforvietnam.org/`). Late entries will not be processed for scoring.
* **Data Ethics Compliance:** Utilizing illegally harvested data, un-anonymized sensitive personal info, or data violating compliance laws will result in immediate disqualification from the scoring round.
